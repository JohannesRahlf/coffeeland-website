## Resources

CoffeeLand follows open science principles, making research outputs openly available to researchers, practitioners, and policymakers.

### Publications

Scientific publications will be made available here as the project progresses. The project aims to produce at least 12 peer-reviewed articles by 2029.

All pre-prints will be deposited on **Zenodo** and **GitHub** under open-access licenses.

### Data & Code

In line with the FAIR data principles, all programming code, scripts, and raw datasets will be made openly available:

- **Code repositories**: Published on GitHub
- **Datasets**: Deposited on Zenodo
- **Remote sensing outputs**: Carbon stock maps, coffee-forest extent maps (available as project progresses)

### Policy Briefs

The project will produce targeted policy briefs for decision-makers in Ethiopia and Norway on:

- Carbon monitoring in coffee-forest landscapes
- Integrating coffee-forests into REDD+ and carbon markets
- Climate-smart shade tree management recommendations

### Data Management

A full Data Management Plan (DMP) has been developed for the project in accordance with Research Council of Norway requirements, ensuring long-term preservation and accessibility of all research data.
